Headline: 
Federal Judge Accuses Trump Administration of Noncompliance Amid Deportation Controversies 

The Trump administration is under scrutiny, accused of intentional noncompliance in the case of Kilmar Abrego Garcia, a man mistakenly deported to El Salvador last month. U.S District Judge Paula Xinis, overseeing the case, criticized the administration for not acting in "good faith" as part of the expedited fact-finding process. Concurrently, President Donald Trump addressed his administration's immigration agenda and the challenges posed by the courts.

According to the 8-page order from Judge Xinis, the Justice Department's handling of the discovery process has not been satisfactory. The Trump administration was accused of using vague and unsubstantiated assertions of privilege as a shield to obstruct discovery and evade compliance with the court's orders. "For weeks, Defendants have sought refuge behind vague and unsubstantiated assertions of privilege," Xinis wrote, ordering the Justice Department lawyers to provide a more "specific legal and factual basis" for invoking privilege. 

Meanwhile, President Trump, speaking at the White House, defended his administration's efforts to crack down on illegal immigration. According to Fox News, he stated, "Honestly, it's one of the great successes; we have virtually nobody coming in illegally." However, he expressed frustration over court rulings hindering his administration's efforts and argued for cooperation from the courts. "We were having hundreds of thousands of people a month come in under Biden...and a judge can say 'No, you have to have a trial â€¦ the trials going to take two years and now we're going to have a very dangerous country if we're not allowed to do what we're entitled to do," the President said.

The President's comments came after Abrego Garcia's attorneys complained about the government's insufficient responses to discovery questions, known as interrogatories, and inadequate documents. Earlier that day, the top lawyer at the Department of Homeland Security, Joseph Mazzara, was deposed by Abrego Garcia's attorneys. 

The President also addressed his administration's ongoing trade war with China, asserting that it is up to China to make a deal work. He assured that the current 145 percent tariff against China would be much lower in whichever deal is ultimately reached, but noted it won't be zero either.

In addition to immigration and trade, President Trump dismissed rumors of intending to fire Jerome Powell from his role as chair of the U.S Federal Reserve over a disagreement about lowering interest rates. "No, I have no intention of firing him," he confirmed.

Despite the differences in emphasis, both sources underscore the ongoing challenges faced by the Trump administration in implementing its immigration policies. The administration's handling of these issues continues to be a subject of controversy and legal scrutiny.
